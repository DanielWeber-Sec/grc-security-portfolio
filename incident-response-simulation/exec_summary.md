# Executive Summary – Incident Response Simulation

## Overview

This project demonstrates how a cybersecurity incident is managed end-to-end — from initial compromise to recovery and organizational learning.

It complements an ISO 27001-oriented ISMS by translating theoretical risks and controls into a realistic incident scenario.

---

## Scenario

A ransomware attack exploits a vulnerable externally exposed system.

Due to missing controls:

- The vulnerability is not detected  
- The attack remains unnoticed  
- The attacker gains access to internal systems  
- Critical data is compromised  

---

## Key Problem

The incident is not caused by a single failure, but by a combination of weaknesses:

- Missing patch management  
- Lack of monitoring and detection  
- Incomplete asset visibility  
- Weak access control (e.g. missing MFA)  

---

## Response Approach

The incident is handled through a structured process:

1. **Detection & Analysis**  
   Identification of the attack and assessment of impact  

2. **Containment**  
   Isolation of affected systems to limit damage  

3. **Eradication**  
   Removal of attacker presence and vulnerabilities  

4. **Recovery**  
   Restoration of systems and operations  

5. **Lessons Learned**  
   Improvement of controls and processes  

---

## Governance & Decision-Making

The project highlights the importance of structured decision-making:

- Clear roles (Incident Commander, Technical Lead, Management)  
- Defined escalation paths  
- Fast, risk-based decisions under pressure  

Key decisions include:

- System shutdown vs. continued operation  
- Communication to stakeholders  
- Handling of ransom demands  

---

## Communication & Regulatory Aspects

Effective incident response requires:

- Controlled internal communication  
- Structured external communication (customers, regulators)  
- Compliance with legal obligations (e.g. GDPR 72h rule)  

---

## Key Insight

Security incidents are not purely technical events.

They require alignment between:

- Technical teams  
- Management  
- Legal / compliance functions  

---

## Business Impact

Without proper controls, incidents can lead to:

- Financial loss  
- Operational disruption  
- Legal consequences  
- Reputational damage  

---

## Key Takeaway

Security is not just about preventing incidents.

It is about:

- Detecting threats early  
- Responding effectively  
- Making informed decisions  
- Continuously improving resilience  

---

## Positioning

This project demonstrates the ability to:

- Translate technical security issues into business risks  
- Structure incident response processes  
- Align security with governance and compliance  

It reflects a practical, risk-based approach to cybersecurity.
