# Incident Command Structure

## Objective

Define clear roles, responsibilities, and decision authority during a security incident.

---

## Core Roles

### Incident Commander

- Overall responsibility for incident handling  
- Coordinates all activities  
- Makes final decisions  

---

### Technical Lead

- Analyzes the attack  
- Defines technical response actions  
- Coordinates containment and remediation  

---

### IT Operations

- Executes technical actions  
- Applies patches and changes  
- Restores systems  

---

### Management

- Makes business-critical decisions  
- Approves major actions (e.g. shutdown, communication)  
- Evaluates financial and legal impact  

---

### Communication / Legal (optional)

- Handles internal and external communication  
- Ensures compliance and legal alignment  

---

## Escalation Flow

1. Incident detected  
2. Technical team validates incident  
3. Incident Commander is assigned  
4. Severity is classified  
5. Management is informed (depending on severity)  

---

## Severity-Based Escalation

### Low

- Handled by technical team  
- No management involvement  

### Medium

- Incident Commander involved  
- Status updates to management  

### High / Critical

- Immediate escalation to management  
- Crisis-level coordination  
- Possible external communication  

---

## Decision Authority

| Decision | Owner |
|--------|------|
| Technical containment | Technical Lead |
| System shutdown | Incident Commander / Management |
| External communication | Management |
| Ransom payment | Executive Management |

---

## Key Principle

During an incident:

- Decisions must be fast  
- Responsibilities must be clear  
- Escalation paths must be predefined  

---

## Key Takeaway

A well-defined command structure reduces:

- Response time  
- Confusion  
- Business impact  

It ensures that technical actions and management decisions are aligned.
