# Communication Matrix

## Objective

Define who communicates with whom, when, and under which conditions during a security incident.

---

## Communication Principles

- Communication must be timely, accurate, and controlled  
- Avoid information overload and confusion  
- Ensure alignment between technical teams and management  
- Maintain a single source of truth  

---

## Internal Communication

| Role | Receives Information From | Communicates To | Purpose |
|-----|--------------------------|-----------------|--------|
| Technical Team | Monitoring / Detection | Incident Commander | Initial analysis |
| Incident Commander | Technical Team | Management | Situation overview |
| Management | Incident Commander | Stakeholders | Business decisions |
| IT Operations | Technical Lead | Technical Team | Execution |

---

## External Communication

| Audience | Responsible | Trigger |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Customers | Management / Communication | Service impact or data breach |
| Regulators | Legal / Compliance | Legal obligation |
| Partners / Vendors | Incident Commander | Operational dependency |
| Public / Media | Communication Team | Reputation impact |

---

## Severity-Based Communication

### Low

- Internal only  
- Technical team handles communication  

---

### Medium

- Incident Commander informed  
- Management receives updates  

---

### High / Critical

- Immediate escalation  
- Management fully involved  
- External communication prepared  

---

## Communication Channels

- Incident War Room (e.g. Teams / Slack)  
- Email (formal updates)  
- Ticketing system (tracking)  
- Executive briefings  

---

## Key Principle

During incidents:

- Information must flow **fast but controlled**  
- Only authorized roles communicate externally  
- Consistency is critical  

---

## Key Takeaway

Poor communication increases damage.

Clear communication:

- Reduces confusion  
- Accelerates response  
- Protects reputation
