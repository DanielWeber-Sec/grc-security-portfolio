# ISO 27001 ↔ DORA Mapping

## Objective

This document demonstrates how ISO/IEC 27001 can support governance and operational resilience requirements related to DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act).

The focus is not on legal interpretation, but on practical governance alignment between an established ISMS and modern ICT risk management expectations.

The purpose is to show how organizations can use structured information security governance to strengthen resilience, improve audit readiness, and support regulatory compliance obligations.

---

## Why This Matters

Financial institutions and critical service providers increasingly operate under multiple regulatory and governance expectations simultaneously.

These often include:

- ISO/IEC 27001
- DORA
- NIS2
- GDPR
- internal governance requirements
- customer and audit obligations

DORA significantly increases expectations around:

- ICT risk management
- operational resilience
- third-party oversight
- incident reporting
- resilience testing
- governance accountability

Organizations with an established ISMS can use ISO 27001 as a structured governance foundation to support many of these requirements.

---

## Governance Perspective

ISO 27001 provides the management system structure for information security governance.

DORA expands the focus toward operational resilience and ICT-related governance obligations in regulated environments.

From a governance perspective:

- ISO 27001 defines how security is managed.
- DORA increases expectations regarding resilience and oversight.
- Governance mapping helps align both into a unified operating model.

The practical goal is not to manage separate compliance silos, but to build integrated governance structures that support multiple regulatory requirements simultaneously.

## Key Alignment Areas

### 1. ICT Risk Management

DORA requires organizations to establish structured ICT risk management frameworks.

ISO 27001 supports this through:

- structured risk assessment processes
- risk treatment planning
- control implementation
- risk ownership
- management review processes
- continual improvement activities

This creates a strong governance foundation for identifying, evaluating, and managing operational ICT risks.

---

### 2. Operational Resilience

Operational resilience is one of the central themes of DORA.

ISO 27001 supports resilience through:

- business continuity planning
- backup and recovery processes
- incident response structures
- crisis management coordination
- operational recovery procedures
- testing and review activities

The governance objective is not only to protect systems, but to maintain critical business operations during disruptive events.

---

### 3. ICT Incident Management

DORA increases expectations around ICT-related incident governance and reporting.

ISO 27001 supports this through structured incident management processes including:

- detection and reporting
- escalation procedures
- response coordination
- communication workflows
- lessons learned activities
- post-incident improvement measures

Well-defined incident governance structures improve transparency, accountability, and operational response capabilities.

---

### 4. Third-Party Risk Governance

DORA places strong emphasis on oversight of external ICT providers and service dependencies.

ISO 27001 supports supplier governance through:

- supplier assessments
- contractual security requirements
- monitoring activities
- access governance
- third-party risk reviews
- service continuity considerations

This is particularly relevant for organizations relying on:

- cloud providers
- managed service providers
- SaaS platforms
- outsourced operational services

---

### 5. Governance Accountability

DORA increases management accountability for ICT risk oversight and resilience governance.

ISO 27001 supports this through:

- leadership commitment
- management review activities
- governance reporting
- role and responsibility definitions
- risk acceptance decisions
- performance monitoring

This creates a direct connection between operational security governance and executive oversight responsibilities.

---

## Executive Interpretation

DORA should not be viewed as an isolated compliance obligation.

Instead, it should be integrated into an organization's broader governance and resilience strategy.

Organizations with mature ISMS structures already possess many foundational governance components required for operational resilience management.

The challenge is often not creating entirely new structures, but:

- strengthening governance maturity
- improving resilience oversight
- increasing reporting capabilities
- formalizing third-party governance
- enhancing operational transparency

Integrated governance models reduce duplicated effort and improve consistency across regulatory expectations.

---

## Example Governance Scenario

A financial services provider already operates an ISO 27001-aligned ISMS and becomes subject to DORA requirements.

Instead of building a completely separate resilience program, the organization can extend its existing governance structures.

Example approach:

1. Review the existing ICT risk management process.
2. Identify DORA-relevant operational resilience obligations.
3. Assess incident escalation and reporting capabilities.
4. Evaluate third-party governance maturity.
5. Expand business continuity and resilience testing activities.
6. Strengthen management reporting and governance oversight.
7. Update policies, procedures, and accountability structures.
8. Integrate resilience monitoring into ongoing governance reviews.

This approach improves operational resilience while reducing governance fragmentation.

## Summary

ISO 27001 and DORA address different perspectives of security governance, but they align strongly in practice.

ISO 27001 provides the management system structure.

DORA strengthens expectations around ICT risk governance, operational resilience, third-party oversight, and management accountability.

Organizations that integrate both perspectives can create stronger governance structures, improve resilience capabilities, and support long-term regulatory readiness.
