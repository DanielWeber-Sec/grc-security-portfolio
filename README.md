Practical GRC & ISMS portfolio — ISO 27001, risk management, and security governance

## GRC & Information Security Portfolio

This repository demonstrates structured, practical work in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) and Information Security Management.

The focus is not on isolated technical controls, but on designing security as a business-aligned management system — enabling risk-based decision-making, clear accountability, and implementable governance structures.

All artifacts are built around a realistic scenario of a mid-sized automotive supplier in Germany, with a strong focus on protecting intellectual property, development environments, and cloud-based collaboration.

The overall logic follows a consistent approach:

risk identification → risk evaluation → control selection → policy design → ISMS structuring

## Governance & Responsibilities

The ISMS follows a clearly defined governance structure:

- Management: accountable for risk acceptance and strategic direction  
- IT / Security: responsible for implementation and monitoring  
- Business Units: responsible for adherence to defined controls  
- Employees: responsible for incident reporting and policy compliance  

Clear ownership ensures accountability and enables effective execution of security measures.

---

## Metrics & Monitoring

The effectiveness of the ISMS is measured through:

- Patch compliance rate  
- Number of detected incidents  
- Mean time to detect (MTTD)  
- Mean time to respond (MTTR)  
- Coverage of monitoring across critical systems  

These metrics enable continuous improvement and provide transparency for management reporting.

## Projects

### ISMS Implementation (ISO 27001 aligned)

Design and structuring of an ISO 27001-oriented Information Security Management System (ISMS) for a mid-sized automotive supplier.

The scenario reflects a realistic environment with:

- high dependency on production uptime  
- integration with external suppliers  
- handling of sensitive operational and customer data  

The ISMS is designed to be practical, scalable, and aligned with business priorities.

Key elements include:

- definition of scope and system boundaries  
- identification of critical assets and dependencies  
- structured risk assessment (impact vs. likelihood)  
- selection and prioritization of relevant controls  
- definition of core security policies  
- creation of a Statement of Applicability (SoA)  

👉 Full project: `/isms-project`

---

### Risk Assessment

A structured risk assessment based on ISO 27001 principles, focusing on business impact and prioritization.

The approach goes beyond threat identification by linking risks to operational and financial consequences.

The assessment includes:

- definition of evaluation criteria (impact and likelihood)  
- consideration of financial, operational, and reputational impact  
- identification of key risk scenarios such as:
  - production downtime  
  - phishing and credential compromise  
  - supplier-related risks  

The outcome is a prioritized risk view that directly informs control selection and management decisions.

👉 Details: `/isms-project/risk-assessment`

---

### Security Policies

Definition of core policies required to support the ISMS.

The policies are intentionally designed to be:

- concise but actionable  
- aligned with ISO 27001 expectations  
- realistic in terms of implementation effort  

Included policies:

- Access Control Policy  
- Backup & Recovery Policy  
- Incident Response Policy  

Each policy focuses on clear responsibilities, principles, and minimum viable controls — avoiding unnecessary complexity.

👉 Policies: `/isms-project/security-policies`

### Tabletop Exercise

Simulation of a ransomware incident in a production-driven environment.

The exercise evaluates:

- incident detection and escalation  
- containment and recovery processes  
- decision-making under pressure  
- communication between stakeholders  
- effectiveness of business continuity measures  

It serves as a validation layer between defined policies and real-world execution capability.

👉 Exercise: `/isms-project/tabletop-exercise`

## Continuous Improvement

## Approach

This portfolio follows a pragmatic and business-oriented approach to security:

- security is treated as part of risk management and business continuity  
- controls are selected based on effectiveness and feasibility  
- documentation is designed to be usable, not just compliant  
- focus on structures that can realistically be implemented in organizations  

The emphasis is on coherence, prioritization, and practical applicability — not theoretical completeness.

---

## Positioning

This portfolio represents a transition from IT project management into information security governance.

The focus lies on bridging business and security, structuring complex environments, and enabling informed, risk-based decisions — rather than purely technical security operations.
