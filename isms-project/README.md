# ISMS Mini Implementation – Automotive Supplier

---

## Overview

This project represents a structured, ISO 27001-aligned Information Security Management System (ISMS) for a mid-sized automotive supplier in Germany.

It demonstrates how information security can be implemented as a governance and risk management discipline, integrating risk assessment, control definition, and incident response into a coherent and business-aligned system.

The focus lies on practical applicability rather than theoretical completeness.

---

## Objective

To design a realistic ISMS that enables:

- identification and prioritization of business-relevant risks  
- structured decision-making based on risk exposure  
- implementation of appropriate and feasible security controls  
- alignment between IT, security, and business stakeholders  

The goal is to reflect how security would be managed in a production-driven environment with high operational dependencies.

---

## Scenario Context

The simulated organization operates in the automotive supply chain and is characterized by:

- high dependency on production uptime  
- integration with external suppliers and partners  
- handling of sensitive design, operational, and customer data  
- increasing reliance on cloud-based collaboration tools  

This environment requires a balanced approach between security, availability, and operational efficiency.

---

## Components

### Risk Assessment

Structured identification and evaluation of risks affecting critical business processes and assets.

Includes:

- risk register (Excel-based)  
- definition of evaluation criteria (likelihood × impact)  
- prioritization of risks based on business impact  
- linkage between risks and corresponding control measures  

The focus is on understanding the business implications of risks, not just their technical characteristics.

---

### Tabletop Exercise

Simulation of a ransomware incident in a production-driven environment.

The exercise is designed to validate:

- incident detection and escalation processes  
- containment and recovery procedures  
- decision-making under pressure  
- communication between technical and business stakeholders  
- effectiveness of business continuity measures  

It serves as a bridge between defined policies and real-world execution capability.

---

### Security Policies

Definition of core governance documents required to support the ISMS.

Included policies:

- Incident Response Policy  
- Access Control Policy  
- Backup & Recovery Policy  

The policies are intentionally designed to be:

- concise and actionable  
- aligned with ISO 27001 expectations  
- realistic in terms of implementation effort  

The emphasis is on clarity, ownership, and enforceability.

---

## Methodology

The ISMS follows a structured, risk-based approach aligned with ISO 27001 principles.

Risk evaluation is based on:

- Likelihood (scale 1–5)  
- Impact (scale 1–5, including operational, financial, and reputational dimensions)  

Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact  

This enables consistent prioritization and supports transparent decision-making.

---

## Outcome

The project results in:

- a structured and prioritized risk landscape  
- a defined set of security controls aligned with business needs  
- a baseline policy framework  
- a validated incident response capability through simulation  

Together, these elements form a coherent and implementable ISMS foundation.

---

## Goal

To demonstrate practical GRC capabilities by connecting:

- risk identification and evaluation  
- control selection and prioritization  
- governance structures and responsibilities  
- incident response and operational readiness  

The emphasis is on building a system that is not only compliant in theory, but usable and effective in practice.
