# Incident Response Policy

---

## Objective

The objective of this policy is to establish a structured and consistent approach for identifying, managing, and resolving information security incidents.

The goal is to minimize business impact, ensure rapid recovery, and maintain compliance with regulatory and organizational requirements.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All IT systems and services
- Production-related systems and supporting infrastructure
- All employees, contractors, and third-party users

It covers all types of security incidents, including:

- Malware and ransomware attacks
- Unauthorized access or privilege misuse
- Data breaches or data leakage
- Service disruptions caused by security events

---

## Definition of a Security Incident

A security incident is any event that compromises or has the potential to compromise:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

Examples include:

- Suspicious login behavior or credential misuse
- Detection of malware or ransomware
- Unauthorized system changes
- Data exfiltration or leakage

---

## Policy

- All security incidents must be reported immediately to IT Security
- Incidents must be assessed and classified based on severity and business impact
- A structured incident response process must be followed:

  - Detection  
  - Containment  
  - Eradication  
  - Recovery  
  - Lessons Learned  

- Evidence must be preserved for forensic analysis
- Communication must be coordinated and controlled
- Critical incidents must be escalated to management without delay

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|--------------|
| IT Security | Incident coordination and technical response |
| IT Operations | System containment and recovery |
| Management | Strategic decision-making and risk acceptance |
| Legal & Compliance | Regulatory reporting and legal assessment |
| Communications | Internal and external communication |

---

## Incident Response Process

### 1. Detection

- Identify potential incidents through monitoring systems (EDR, logging, alerts)
- Validate whether an event qualifies as a security incident

---

### 2. Containment

- Isolate affected systems
- Prevent lateral movement
- Disable compromised accounts where necessary

---

### 3. Eradication

- Remove malicious artifacts
- Close vulnerabilities and apply patches
- Eliminate persistence mechanisms

---

### 4. Recovery

- Restore systems from verified backups
- Validate system integrity and functionality
- Resume business operations in a controlled manner

---

### 5. Lessons Learned

- Conduct post-incident analysis
- Identify root causes and control weaknesses
- Improve processes and controls

---

## Communication

- All incidents must be reported immediately
- Management must be informed for high-severity incidents
- External communication must be coordinated with Legal and Communications

---

## Escalation Criteria

Escalation is required if:

- Business operations are disrupted
- Sensitive or regulated data is affected
- Legal or regulatory obligations are triggered

---

## Documentation and Logging

Each incident must be documented, including:

- Timeline of events
- Actions taken
- Decisions made
- Impact and outcome
