# NIS2 Mapping

---

## Objective

Demonstrate how the ISMS aligns with key requirements of the NIS2 Directive.

---

## Approach

NIS2 requirements are addressed through existing ISMS components, with additional focus on governance, accountability, and reporting obligations.

---

## Key Requirements & ISMS Mapping

| NIS2 Requirement            | ISMS Implementation                            |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Risk Management Measures    | Risk assessment and treatment processes        |
| Incident Handling           | Incident Response Policy and Tabletop Exercise |
| Business Continuity         | Backup Policy and Recovery Planning            |
| Supply Chain Security       | Supplier risk management controls              |
| Governance & Accountability | Defined roles and responsibilities             |
| Monitoring & Detection      | Logging and monitoring controls                |

---

## Key Differences to ISO 27001

- NIS2 introduces mandatory reporting obligations
- stronger management accountability
- regulatory enforcement and penalties

---

## Implementation Perspective

The ISMS provides the structural foundation, while NIS2 defines additional regulatory expectations.

Compliance is achieved by extending governance processes and ensuring reporting capabilities.

---

## Conclusion

An ISO 27001-based ISMS can effectively support NIS2 compliance when extended with regulatory-specific requirements.
