# Vendor Classification Model

## Objective

This document demonstrates a governance-oriented vendor classification approach used to prioritize third-party suppliers based on risk exposure, operational dependency, and security relevance.

The objective is to support:

- risk-based supplier governance
- operational resilience
- audit readiness
- regulatory alignment
- proportional security oversight

A structured classification model helps organizations allocate governance resources according to supplier criticality and business impact.

---

## Why Vendor Classification Matters

Not all suppliers introduce the same level of risk.

Some vendors may have:

- privileged access to internal systems
- access to sensitive data
- operational dependencies
- regulatory relevance
- critical infrastructure responsibilities

Without structured classification, organizations may:

- apply inconsistent controls
- overlook critical suppliers
- misallocate governance resources
- underestimate operational dependencies
- weaken resilience capabilities

A governance-oriented classification model helps organizations identify which suppliers require enhanced oversight and monitoring.

---

## Governance Perspective

Vendor classification is not purely a procurement exercise.

It is part of enterprise risk governance.

The classification process should support:

- supplier prioritization
- governance accountability
- risk treatment decisions
- contractual security requirements
- resilience planning
- audit and compliance activities

The level of oversight should be proportionate to supplier risk exposure and business criticality.

---

## Example Vendor Classification Model

| Classification | Description | Governance Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | High operational dependency or privileged access to critical systems and sensitive information | Highest |
| High | Significant business impact or access to important operational environments | High |
| Medium | Limited operational dependency with moderate security relevance | Medium |
| Low | Minimal operational or security impact | Low |

---

## Example Classification Criteria

| Governance Criteria | Example Considerations |
|---|---|
| Data Access | Access to sensitive, personal, financial, or operational data |
| System Access | VPN access, privileged accounts, administrative access |
| Operational Dependency | Supplier supports critical business services |
| Regulatory Impact | Potential compliance or reporting implications |
| Availability Impact | Service disruption affects operations significantly |
| Network Connectivity | Direct integration into internal environments |
| Security Maturity | Certifications, governance maturity, security controls |
| Incident Exposure | Potential impact during security incidents or outages |

---

## Governance Actions by Classification

| Classification | Example Governance Activities |
|---|---|
| Critical | Enhanced due diligence, executive oversight, resilience reviews, regular audits |
| High | Formal risk assessments, periodic security reviews, contractual controls |
| Medium | Standard supplier review process and basic governance controls |
| Low | Lightweight review and procurement-level oversight |

---

## Example Governance Workflow

1. Identify supplier relationship.
2. Assess operational dependency and data exposure.
3. Evaluate access levels and regulatory relevance.
4. Assign risk classification.
5. Define governance requirements and review frequency.
6. Document ownership and accountability.
7. Monitor supplier changes and reassess periodically.

This process helps ensure supplier governance remains aligned with business and operational risk exposure.

## Executive Interpretation

A mature organization should not apply identical governance controls to every supplier.

Instead, governance activities should scale according to:

- operational dependency
- business criticality
- security exposure
- resilience impact
- regulatory relevance

A structured vendor classification model enables organizations to focus governance efforts where supplier-related risks are highest.

---

## Summary

Vendor classification is a foundational component of Third-Party Risk Management.

It helps organizations prioritize governance activities, improve operational resilience, support regulatory alignment, and establish proportional oversight across supplier relationships.

The purpose of this document is to demonstrate how structured supplier classification can support integrated enterprise security governance and risk management.
