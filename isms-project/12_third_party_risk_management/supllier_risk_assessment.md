# Supplier Risk Assessment

## Objective

This document demonstrates a governance-oriented supplier risk assessment approach used to identify, evaluate, and manage risks associated with third-party relationships.

The objective is to support:

- operational resilience
- supplier governance
- risk-informed decision-making
- audit readiness
- regulatory alignment
- enterprise security governance

The assessment process helps organizations identify supplier-related risks and define proportional governance measures based on operational and security impact.

---

## Why Supplier Risk Assessments Matter

Third-party suppliers may introduce risks related to:

- sensitive data exposure
- operational disruption
- privileged access
- supply chain compromise
- regulatory violations
- service availability
- security governance weaknesses

Without structured assessments, organizations may:

- underestimate supplier dependencies
- overlook operational risks
- apply inconsistent governance controls
- weaken resilience capabilities
- increase audit and compliance exposure

A structured supplier risk assessment process improves visibility, accountability, and governance consistency across external relationships.

---

## Governance Perspective

Supplier risk assessments are part of enterprise risk governance.

The purpose is not only to identify technical risks, but also to evaluate:

- operational dependency
- governance maturity
- resilience impact
- regulatory exposure
- contractual risk
- business continuity implications

A mature governance approach should ensure supplier risks are documented, reviewed, treated, and periodically reassessed.

---

## Governance Workflow

1. Identify supplier relationship and service scope.
2. Determine supplier classification and criticality.
3. Assess operational and security risks.
4. Evaluate likelihood and impact.
5. Define mitigation measures and governance controls.
6. Assign ownership and accountability.
7. Document residual risk.
8. Schedule periodic reassessment and review.

This process supports risk-informed supplier governance and operational resilience management.

| Governance Control | Objective |
|---|---|

| Contractual Security Clauses | Define minimum security expectations |
| Access Restrictions | Limit unnecessary access exposure |
| MFA Requirements | Strengthen authentication security |
| Incident Notification Requirements | Improve response coordination |
| Periodic Reviews | Reassess supplier risk posture |
| Audit Rights | Support governance transparency |

---

## Residual Risk Perspective

Not all supplier risks can be fully eliminated.

After mitigation activities are implemented, organizations should evaluate:

- remaining operational exposure
- residual security risk
- resilience impact
- business acceptance decisions
- management accountability

Residual risks should be documented and periodically reviewed within the broader ISMS and governance structure.

## Executive Interpretation

Supplier risk management should not be treated as a one-time compliance exercise.

It should function as a continuous governance process integrated into broader enterprise risk management and resilience planning activities.

Organizations that understand supplier dependencies, governance maturity, and operational exposure are better positioned to reduce disruption risks and improve long-term resilience capabilities.
