# Third-Party Review Process

## Objective

This document demonstrates a governance-oriented third-party review process used to monitor, reassess, and manage supplier-related risks throughout the supplier lifecycle.

The objective is to support:

- continuous supplier oversight
- operational resilience
- governance accountability
- audit readiness
- risk-informed decision-making
- regulatory alignment

A structured review process helps organizations ensure that supplier relationships remain aligned with security, resilience, and governance expectations over time.

---

## Why Continuous Supplier Reviews Matter

Supplier risks are not static.

Changes in:

- operational dependencies
- security posture
- business processes
- technologies
- regulatory requirements
- subcontractor usage
- threat landscapes

can significantly alter supplier-related risk exposure.

Without periodic reviews, organizations may:

- overlook emerging supplier risks
- lose visibility into governance weaknesses
- weaken operational resilience
- increase compliance exposure
- maintain outdated assumptions about supplier security maturity

Continuous oversight improves governance transparency and supports long-term resilience management.

---

## Governance Perspective

Third-party reviews are part of ongoing enterprise governance.

The purpose is not only to verify technical controls, but also to evaluate whether suppliers continue to meet organizational expectations regarding:

- security governance
- operational resilience
- access management
- incident handling
- regulatory alignment
- contractual obligations
- business continuity capabilities

Review activities should scale according to supplier criticality and operational relevance.

## Example Review Triggers

| Trigger Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Scheduled Review | Annual or quarterly governance review |
| Security Incident | Supplier-related breach or operational disruption |
| Contract Renewal | Reassessment before extending agreements |
| Operational Changes | Major service or infrastructure changes |
| Regulatory Changes | New compliance or governance requirements |
| Access Changes | Increased privileged access exposure |
| Risk Reassessment | Significant changes in risk profile |

---

## Example Review Areas

| Governance Area | Example Review Activities |
|---|---|
| Security Governance | Review updated policies and governance structures |
| Access Management | Validate privileged access reviews and MFA usage |
| Incident Management | Review incident escalation and communication procedures |
| Business Continuity | Validate recovery and resilience capabilities |
| Compliance Alignment | Review regulatory and contractual obligations |
| Supplier Dependencies | Assess subcontractor and fourth-party exposure |
| Vulnerability Management | Review remediation and patch management maturity |
| Operational Changes | Assess impact of major infrastructure or service changes |

---

## Example Third-Party Review Workflow

1. Identify supplier review scope and objectives.
2. Confirm supplier classification and criticality.
3. Review existing risk assessments and governance records.
4. Validate updated governance and security evidence.
5. Assess changes in operational or regulatory exposure.
6. Document identified risks or governance gaps.
7. Define remediation requirements if necessary.
8. Update governance records and review schedules.

This process supports continuous governance visibility and supplier risk oversight.

---

## Example Governance Outcomes

| Review Result | Example Governance Action |
|---|---|
| No Significant Changes | Maintain existing governance approach |
| Minor Governance Gaps | Define remediation activities |
| Increased Operational Risk | Escalate review frequency |
| Elevated Security Exposure | Require additional controls |
| Critical Governance Weaknesses | Escalate to management review |

---

## Example Review Frequency Model

| Supplier Classification | Example Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Critical | Quarterly |
| High | Semi-Annual |
| Medium | Annual |
| Low | Periodic or event-driven |

Review frequency should remain proportional to operational dependency and risk exposure.

## Executive Interpretation

Third-Party Risk Management should not end after supplier onboarding.

A mature governance model requires continuous visibility into supplier-related operational, security, and resilience risks throughout the supplier lifecycle.

Periodic review processes help organizations maintain governance transparency, strengthen resilience capabilities, and adapt to changing operational environments.

---

## Summary

Continuous supplier reviews are an essential component of Third-Party Risk Management.

They help organizations reassess supplier-related risks, validate governance maturity, improve operational resilience, and support ongoing regulatory readiness.

The purpose of this document is to demonstrate how structured supplier review processes can support integrated enterprise security governance and operational resilience objectives.
