## Incident Response Plan

### Objective

This plan defines the operational coordination of incident response activities, including escalation, communication, and decision-making.

---

### Incident Severity Levels

| Level  | Description            | Example                  |
|--------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Low    | Minor issue           | Single infected device   |
| Medium | Limited disruption    | Partial system outage    |
| High   | Major incident        | Ransomware attack        |
| Critical | Business-critical failure | Full production stop |

---

### Escalation Matrix

| Severity | Escalation Target         | Timeframe |
|----------|--------------------------|----------|
| Low      | IT Team                  | Immediate |
| Medium   | IT + IT Manager          | < 30 min  |
| High     | IT + Management          | < 15 min  |
| Critical | C-Level + Crisis Team    | Immediate |

---

### Roles & Contacts

| Role          | Responsibility |
|---------------|----------------|
| IT Operations | Technical response |
| CISO / Security Lead | Coordination |
| Management    | Business decisions |
| Legal         | Regulatory obligations |
| Communication | External communication |

---

### Communication Plan

- Internal escalation via defined channels (Teams, Phone)
- Management briefing for high/critical incidents
- External communication (customers, authorities) if required

---

### Decision Triggers

- Shutdown systems → if spread risk is high  
- Activate BCM → if critical systems unavailable  
- Notify authorities → if personal data affected  

---

### Key Questions During Incident

- What systems are affected?  
- What is the business impact?  
- Can we contain it quickly?  
- Do we need external support?
