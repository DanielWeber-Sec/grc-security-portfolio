# Risk Assessment Methodology

---

## Objective

The objective of this methodology is to provide a structured, transparent, and repeatable approach for evaluating information security risks within the defined ISMS scope.

It ensures that risks are assessed consistently and prioritized according to their potential business impact.

---

## Assessment Approach

Risks are evaluated using a qualitative scoring model based on two dimensions:

- Likelihood (1–5)
- Impact (1–5)

The overall risk score is calculated as:

**Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact**

This approach supports pragmatic prioritization and enables comparison across different risk scenarios.

---

## Likelihood Scale

| Score | Description    | Explanation                                                 |
| ----- | -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Rare           | Highly unlikely to occur                                    |
| 2     | Unlikely       | Possible, but not expected under normal conditions          |
| 3     | Possible       | Could occur under realistic circumstances                   |
| 4     | Likely         | Expected to occur in some situations                        |
| 5     | Almost Certain | Very likely to occur repeatedly or under current conditions |

---

## Impact Scale

Impact is assessed from a business perspective and may include operational, financial, and reputational consequences.

| Score | Description | Explanation                                                |
| ----- | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Negligible  | Minimal disruption, no significant business impact         |
| 2     | Minor       | Limited operational or financial impact                    |
| 3     | Moderate    | Noticeable disruption requiring management attention       |
| 4     | Major       | Significant operational, financial, or reputational impact |
| 5     | Critical    | Severe business disruption with major consequences         |

---

## Risk Levels

| Risk Score | Risk Level | Interpretation                              |
| ---------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1–5        | Low        | Acceptable with limited monitoring          |
| 6–10       | Medium     | Requires evaluation and possible treatment  |
| 11–15      | High       | Requires treatment and management attention |
| 16–25      | Critical   | Immediate action required                   |

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Risks are assessed based on realistic threat scenarios affecting the defined scope.

The assessment takes into account:

- business process dependency
- system criticality
- exposure to internal and external threats
- potential operational disruption
- possible financial and reputational consequences

---

## Documentation

Each identified risk is documented in the risk register, including:

- risk description
- affected asset or process
- likelihood score
- impact score
- resulting risk score
- proposed treatment approach
- ownership and status

---

## Outcome

This methodology provides the basis for:

- structured risk prioritization
- consistent treatment decisions
- traceability between risks, controls, and management actions
