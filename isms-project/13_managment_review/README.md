# Management Review & Governance Oversight

> Governance-oriented management review structure focused on executive oversight, operational resilience, audit readiness, and continual ISMS improvement.

---

# Executive Overview

This section demonstrates how management review activities can support governance visibility, risk-informed decision-making, operational resilience, and continual improvement within an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

The focus is not purely operational reporting.

Instead, this section demonstrates how organizations can establish structured executive oversight capabilities across:

- risk governance  
- operational resilience  
- supplier governance  
- audit readiness  
- compliance alignment  
- security performance monitoring  
- continual improvement activities  

This management review layer represents the executive governance perspective of the broader ISMS implementation.

---

# Governance Perspective

Modern security governance requires more than technical controls and operational processes.

Organizations increasingly require executive-level visibility into:

- risk exposure  
- resilience maturity  
- supplier dependencies  
- incident trends  
- audit findings  
- governance effectiveness  
- compliance readiness  
- operational performance indicators  

Management review processes help ensure that security governance remains aligned with business objectives, operational realities, and regulatory expectations.

---

# Governance Objectives

The primary objectives of management review activities include:

- improving executive governance visibility  
- supporting risk-informed decisions  
- monitoring governance effectiveness  
- identifying improvement opportunities  
- strengthening operational resilience  
- supporting audit readiness  
- improving accountability structures  
- tracking governance performance indicators

# Executive Accountability

Management review processes help ensure that governance responsibilities remain visible and actionable across the organization.

This includes:

- leadership accountability  
- governance ownership  
- risk acceptance decisions  
- prioritization of remediation activities  
- continual improvement oversight  

Effective governance requires active management engagement rather than isolated security operations.

---

# Continual Improvement Perspective

An ISMS should evolve continuously as:

- risks change  
- operational dependencies evolve  
- suppliers change  
- incidents occur  
- regulations mature  
- business priorities shift  

Management review activities help organizations maintain governance visibility and adapt security structures accordingly.

# Regulatory Alignment

### ISO/IEC 27001

Supports management review activities, continual improvement, governance accountability, and ISMS effectiveness monitoring.

### NIS2

Strengthens expectations around management accountability and governance oversight.

### DORA

Emphasizes operational resilience governance and executive-level oversight of ICT-related risks.
